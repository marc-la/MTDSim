### Threat-model formalism and the adaptive-knowledge variant

**Source locator:** §III-A (Threat Model), p. 3 (markdown lines 97–105); Table I summarising the three threat models referenced at §III-A line 73; §IV-D (MTD-AD Against Adaptive Adversaries), p. 6 (markdown lines 323–345).

**Paraphrase:** The threat model is a goal/knowledge/capability formalism with three discrete attacker variants distinguished by knowledge level: grey-box (knows extracted features, not the detection algorithm; bounded to the last 10% of benign training data), white-box (knows both features and detection algorithm), and adaptive-knowledge (matches grey-box knowledge of features, plus awareness that MTD-AD is deployed and a general understanding of its strategy — explicitly framed as a Kerckhoffs analogue). Capability is fixed across variants: sniff and replay packets within the network. The goal is fixed: bypass the NIDS to land Denial-of-Service (DoS) flooding (SYN, ACK, UDP) against IoT victims. The adaptive-knowledge variant is realised in §IV-D as a single design-time parameter sweep — the surrogate-model threshold is reduced to a fraction of the original (90%, 70%, 50%, 30%, 10%), pushing adversarial examples further from the boundary so they remain evasive under boundary perturbation; the attacker neither observes the defender at runtime nor updates its policy in-loop.

**Quote (if essential):**
> "Adaptive-knowledge (AK): The attacker assumes the same knowledge as the GB model but is aware that MTD-AD is implemented as a defense mechanism for the NIDS. However, the attacker lacks detailed information about MTD-AD and possesses only a general understanding of its strategy. This is similar to Kerckhoff's principle in cryptosystems." (§III-A, markdown line 85)

**Maps to:** [`../sources/LIT_REVIEW.md`](../sources/LIT_REVIEW.md) §IV-B Table II (line 206 — adaptive ✓\*, stealthy ✓, fidelity Parametric) · [`../sources/LIT_REVIEW.md`](../sources/LIT_REVIEW.md) lines 187–191 (per-paper discussion) · [`../specs/architecture.md`](../specs/architecture.md) §(j) (the methodological-positioning paragraph naming behavioural fidelity as the discriminator the field's evaluations omit).

**Disposition for this thesis:** **contrasted-against** — the formalism is the cross-section's high-water mark for defence-aware attacker modelling, and §IV-B uses that high-water mark to bound how much credit the cross-section deserves. Adaptive-knowledge as a design-time *knowledge specification* is methodologically suggestive (a threat model that names what the attacker knows about the defence is more than the lineage four offer), but the operationalisation collapses to a swept scalar — `threshold_fraction ∈ {0.9, 0.7, 0.5, 0.3, 0.1}` — rather than a runtime decision rule. This thesis's behaviourally-grounded attacker (L2 GASP → L3 OGASP) sits at a different rung of the fidelity descriptor (procedural at minimum, behavioural where motivation-conditioning and TTP-grounding are realised) over a different attack class (multi-stage APT compromise, not single-family ML evasion). The methodological move *toward* a defence-aware attacker transfers; the specific operationalisation does not.

### Semantic-scope contrast: "MTD" as boundary-movement at the model layer

**Source locator:** Abstract (markdown line 5); §I (Introduction), p. 1 (markdown line 31); §II-C (NIDS Adversarial Defences), p. 2 (markdown line 79); §IV-B (MTD-AD), p. 5 (markdown line 163); Fig. 2 caption and §IV-B mutation-technique catalogue (markdown lines 153, 181–193).

**Paraphrase:** "Moving Target Defence" in MTD-AD denotes stochastic alteration of the *NIDS model's decision boundary*, not the network configuration the canonical MTD literature targets (IP shuffling, port hopping, service migration). The architecture maintains a pool of mutated Kitsune variants generated by three model-level mutation techniques — Feature Mapper Mutation (re-cluster the feature mapper, retrain), Model Parameter Mutation (Gaussian-fuzzed / shuffled / switched weights, no retrain), Detection Model Mutation (swap among Kitsune / SOM / LOF / OCSVM with randomised hyperparameters) — filters for false-positive rate, and selects one model at runtime after every n=1,000 packets. The MTD framing is invoked explicitly via citations to Cho et al.'s MTD survey and a Zhang et al. AI-MTD survey (refs [14], [15] in-paper, [`docs/sources/3_2_he2025MTD-AD.md`](../sources/3_2_he2025MTD-AD.md) line 31), and the intent — increase the attacker's cost by continually moving the attack surface — is the canonical MTD intent applied at a different layer of the stack.

**Quote (if essential):**
> "MTD-AD stochastically changes the NIDS's decision boundary, compelling the attacker to create a universally evasive example that can bypass all variants of intrusion detection models, which is considerably harder." (§I, markdown line 31)

**Maps to:** [`../specs/architecture.md`](../specs/architecture.md) §(a) (goal/scope — the thesis's MTD scope is network/host substrate transformations, not model-layer perturbations) · [`../sources/LIT_REVIEW.md`](../sources/LIT_REVIEW.md) §IV-B line 191 (the "attack class is detection-evasion of an ML classifier … not the APT-style network compromise the ladder is built around" qualifier).

**Disposition for this thesis:** **contrasted-against** — semantic-scope contrast, not a correctness claim. MTD-AD's choice to apply the MTD principle at the model layer is a legitimate transposition of the framing; the contrast that matters for this dissertation is that "stealthy" in MTD-AD's threat model means "the adversarial example does not trip a single-stage anomaly detector," whereas in the §IV-B Pyramid-of-Pain framing (Bianco; Sadlek) "stealthy" means "operates at the TTP tier across a multi-stage campaign." The Table II ✓ for stealth carries the lit review's footnote-level caveat that the two senses are not interchangeable. The model-mutation mechanics (FM/MP/DM, the false-positive-rate filter, the diversity-versus-accuracy trade-off finding) and the IoT-intrusion dataset (UQ-IoT-IDS-2021) are out of scope for this dissertation's L1–L3 pipeline.

## Open questions / things to verify

- §III-A's adaptive-knowledge variant is realised in §IV-D by sweeping a single surrogate-model threshold fraction. The §IV-B placement reads this as design-time-parametric. Worth confirming, when the thesis discusses fidelity-descriptor edge cases, whether *any* knowledge-specified attacker that does not update at runtime should sit above parametric — or whether the variant simply *names* a knowledge condition the parametric attacker satisfies (current §IV-B reading).
