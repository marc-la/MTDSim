### Relevance class

**C — Contrast / adjacent.** Kim et al. is one of the five §IV-B cross-section points in [`../sources/LIT_REVIEW.md`](../sources/LIT_REVIEW.md) (§IV-B item 5, line 193); it is cited specifically to anchor the *scripted-fidelity* row of Table II for a CKC-aware MTD defender. The contrast is in *scoping resolution* — Kim et al. organise both defence and attacker behaviour at the Cyber-Kill-Chain phase level, whereas this dissertation's L1–L2 pipeline ([`../specs/architecture.md`](../specs/architecture.md) §(d)–(e)) operates at ATT&CK technique granularity. The paper does not inform an L0→L4 design decision; it bounds the comparison Table II makes against the cross-section.

### CKC-phase scoping of the MTDID defender and attacker

**Source locator:** §1 Introduction (framework framing); §4.3 (CKC seven-step enumeration); §4.4 + Table 3 (CKC × MTD sub-technique matrix); §5.2 + §6.1.1 (attack model and Poisson workload).

**Paraphrase:** MTDID organises proactive defence by mapping shuffle / diversity / redundancy sub-techniques onto digital artefacts (extended from MITRE D3FEND across the virtualisation architecture and TCP/IP stack) and then onto the seven CKC phases (reconnaissance, weaponisation, delivery, exploitation, installation, C2, actions on objectives). ATT&CK techniques are pulled in *taxonomically*: Table 2 lists representative ATT&CK technique IDs (e.g., T1016, T1190, T1059, T1071, T1547) bucketed under each CKC phase to populate the matrix cells, and Table 3 then marks (△) which MTD sub-technique covers which (phase, ATT&CK-ID) pair. ATT&CK provides the technique vocabulary; CKC supplies the structural ordering. The evaluation collapses this further. §5 explicitly notes that "the overlap in time between each phase is out of scope" (§5.2), Weaponisation is excluded ("the stage of weaponization can be ignored because the exploit is open in the public database", §5.2), and the seven CKC phases are folded into three measurement categories — REC, DEI (delivery+exploitation+installation), C2A (§6.1.2). The realised attack is a single automated script targeting one CVE (CVE-2021-41773 path-traversal/RCE on Apache httpd 2.4.49) with arrivals drawn from a Poisson process at rate 0.2/min (§6.1.1), the security metrics computed phase-by-phase (`ASP_REC`, `AST_DEI`, `ART_C2A`, etc.) over those collapsed categories.

**Quote (if essential):**
> "We assume that scenario-based attacks conduct the activities in phases of the CKC … This work proposes a single attack scenario and follows sequential steps. The overlap in time between each phase is out of scope." (§5.2)

**Maps to:** [`../specs/architecture.md`](../specs/architecture.md) §(d) (L1/GAP operates at ATT&CK technique resolution, with edges encoding inter-technique dependency rather than CKC-phase membership) · [`../specs/architecture.md`](../specs/architecture.md) §(e) (L2/GASP conditions on motivation-mediated subgraphs, not on phase-bucketed technique lists) · [`../sources/LIT_REVIEW.md`](../sources/LIT_REVIEW.md) §IV-B item 5 (line 193) and Table II row for Kim et al. (line 207) — scripted fidelity, no persistence / adaptivity / stealth / incentive-awareness.

**Disposition for this thesis:** **contrasted-against.** The contrast is one of *scoping resolution*, not correctness: CKC phases are a coarser quantisation of the same attacker behaviour ATT&CK techniques describe at finer grain. MTDID demonstrates that the phase-level grouping is *adequate for mapping defender coverage* (which mechanism mitigates which broad phase) and is honest about what its evaluation gives up — sequential, non-overlapping phases driven by a single CVE script — but does not reach behavioural fidelity at either resolution. This dissertation operates one rung finer: ATT&CK techniques as the unit of attacker behaviour and the dependency graph between them as the structural carrier, so that an attacker reasons over individual techniques (and their inter-technique preconditions) rather than over phase buckets. The §IV-B Table II placement (scripted) follows from the executed attack model (single CVE, automated script, collapsed phases) rather than from the framework's matrix breadth.
