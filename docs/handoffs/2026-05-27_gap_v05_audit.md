# Audit the GAP v0.5 — reconcile "thin on generalisability", and decide whether to supplement the corpus with literature-grounded paths

This handoff *is* the audit. It characterises GAP v0.5 from the artefact and the
visualisations, diagnoses why it is thin where it is thin, fixes the meaning of
"edge weight", and frames the one methodological decision the next session must
land: **keep the GAP purely observed, or supplement it with inferred APT paths**.
The reasoning is laid out so the next session starts from a position, not a
blank page.

## State of play — what GAP v0.5 actually is

Current artefact ([`../../data/gap/gap_v0.5.json`](../../data/gap/gap_v0.5.json),
Enterprise-only build): **124 technique nodes, 478 edges, 40 source flows.** Two
readings, both rendered under [`../../data/gap/_viz/`](../../data/gap/_viz/) by
[`../../data/gap/_viz/gap_viz.py`](../../data/gap/_viz/gap_viz.py) (untracked
scratch tool):

- **Technique level** — `gap_v0.5_obs2.svg` (recurring backbone, clustered by
  tactic, kill-chain L→R) and `gap_v0.5.svg` (all 478 edges).
- **Tactic FSM** — `gap_v0.5_tactic_fsm_obs4.svg` (backbone) and the full
  `gap_v0.5_tactic_fsm.svg`; techniques collapsed onto their 15 ATT&CK tactics.

The structural facts (recomputed against this build) already live in
[`../notes/2026-05-27_gap_construction.md`](../notes/2026-05-27_gap_construction.md)
§"What the assembled graph looks like". This audit extends that note from *what
the graph looks like* to *what it can and cannot be used to claim*.

The headline numbers this audit turns on:

| Measure | Value | Reading |
|---|---|---|
| Edges seen in exactly 1 flow | 419 / 478 (**88%**) | the long tail is campaign-specific, not general |
| Edges seen in ≥ 3 flows | **7** | the genuinely recurring backbone is tiny |
| Distinct tactic transitions present | 132 / 225 (**59%**) | tactic graph is near-complete → low information in topology |
| Flows containing a **reconnaissance** node | **10 / 40** | the early kill chain is barely observed |
| Flows containing an **initial-access** node | 30 / 40 | the intrusion point *is* well observed |
| `reconnaissance → initial-access` edges | **1** (obs 1) | the "invariant prefix" link is essentially absent |
| Flows with exfiltration / impact / **both** | 13 / 13 / **3** | objectives diverge — flows pursue one or the other |

## What the audit found

### 1. "Thin on generalisability" holds — and it is a property of the corpus, not a bug

The prior characterisation is correct and now quantified: **88% of edges are
single-observation**; only **7 of 478** appear in three or more incidents. The
"recurring adversary behaviour" the name *Generalised* APT Profile promises lives
in a handful of edges — the credential→lateral→execution spine
(`T1003→T1021→T1059`, all obs 4), the tooling loop (`T1105→T1059`, `T1059→T1105`),
and the C2↔discovery beacon-and-recon loop at the tactic level.

Crucially, this is **not** an implementation defect. The build upholds the
no-synthesis invariant ([`../specs/01_gap_schema.md`](../specs/01_gap_schema.md)
§(a)): every edge is one an analyst drew. The GAP is *faithfully* thin because
its input is thin — 40 flows is a small sample, and (see §2) the sample is biased
toward the observable middle of the kill chain. A correct build of a thin corpus
is a thin graph. The lever for thickening it honestly is corpus growth — which is
exactly why the construction note flags the single-observation share as the gauge
of whether "generalised" is earned yet.

### 2. The recon → initial-access puzzle: an observability bias, not a counter-example to the literature

The puzzle: Alshamrani 2019 ([`../extractions/alshamrani2019.md`](../extractions/alshamrani2019.md))
consolidates the APT lifecycle into five phases and makes the structural claim
that **phases 1–2 (reconnaissance, establish-foothold) are invariant** across all
APT operations while **phases 3–5 are objective-conditioned**. If recon and
foothold are invariant, why is there essentially no `reconnaissance →
initial-access` edge in the GAP (1 edge, obs 1)?

The data answers it: **reconnaissance appears in only 10 of 40 flows**, while
**initial-access appears in 30 of 40**. There is no strong recon→initial-access
link because *there is almost no recon in the corpus to link from*. And the reason
is structural, not a modelling error:

- **Attack Flow is built from incident reports / post-hoc CTI.** Reporting starts
  at the point of detection — the intrusion (initial-access) — and works forward.
- **Pre-intrusion reconnaissance is invisible to incident responders.** It happens
  on attacker infrastructure and via OSINT; it leaves little defender-side
  telemetry, so analysts rarely draw it, and when they do it is unanchored
  (`T1593` Search Victim-Owned Websites, `T1595` Active Scanning) rather than
  causally wired to the specific initial-access vector that followed.

So the corpus systematically **under-observes exactly the phase Alshamrani calls
invariant**. The literature is not contradicted — it is *confirmed at the half it
can see*: foothold (initial-access, 30/40) and the mid-chain are densely observed;
the pre-intrusion prefix is not. This is a **survivorship-/observability-bias**
property of incident-derived intelligence, and it is the single most important
thing to understand about what the GAP can represent.

### 3. The corpus *does* corroborate Alshamrani's objective-conditioned suffix

The same lens validates the back half of Alshamrani's claim. Of the 40 flows,
**13 reach exfiltration, 13 reach impact, but only 3 reach both** — most campaigns
commit to *one* terminal objective. That is precisely the "stages 4–5 split by
objective" structure, observed directly in the data. This is a genuine strength:
the GAP's terminal divergence is real and matches theory, which is what makes the
L2 motivation-subgraphing design (architecture §(e) — different motivations select
different terminal regions of the GAP) defensible against this corpus.

### 4. What "edge weight" means — and what it does not

The user's reading is correct, and worth pinning precisely because it is easy to
over-claim downstream. `observation_count` is **the number of distinct incidents
in which an analyst drew that specific technique→technique dependency.** It is:

- a **frequency-of-observation / recurrence** signal — "how commonly this
  dependency was *drawn* across reported incidents";
- grounded in the analyst's **sequencing + dependency** judgement (Attack Flow's
  "to do B the adversary first needed A"), so it carries the analyst's causal read
  of an *observed* incident.

It is emphatically **not**:

- **efficacy / success probability** — Attack Flow records what happened in
  incidents that were notable enough to report; failed attempts and abandoned
  branches are not in the corpus, so weight cannot speak to whether a step
  "works";
- a **transition probability** — weights are unnormalised counts, not a stochastic
  matrix; a node's out-edges do not sum to anything, and the graph is not a Markov
  chain;
- **causal effect strength** — it is observed co-dependency, not a measured
  intervention.

A one-line gloss for the docs: **weight = how often analysts drew this dependency
across reported incidents — a popularity/recurrence measure of observed workflow,
biased toward successful and well-documented campaigns, not a measure of how
likely or how effective the step is.**

### 5. Strengths of this kind of GAP

- **Defensible provenance.** Every edge traces to an analyst-drawn flow; the
  no-synthesis invariant means the simulated attacker does things real adversaries
  were *observed* doing — the claim the whole pipeline rests on.
- **Real structure, not a linear chain.** It preserves AND/OR joins, branches and
  cycles (the Tesla AND-join; the C2↔discovery loop), which a kill-chain ordering
  flattens away.
- **A small but real generalisation signal.** The 7-edge backbone is genuinely
  cross-campaign behaviour, and the terminal objective-divergence (§3) matches the
  literature.
- **Clean supplementation seam.** The lossless per-flow layer is hand-editable by
  design (§01_gap_schema (c)), so adding curated incidents aggregates identically
  to corpus-derived ones — supplementation is architecturally cheap *if* we decide
  to do it (§ central question).

### 6. Limitations

- **Observability bias** (§2): early kill-chain phases (recon, resource-dev)
  under-represented; the GAP is densest in the observable middle.
- **Thin generalisation** (§1): 88% single-observation; statistically the profile
  generalises over a small, possibly unrepresentative sample of 40 incidents.
- **Near-complete, low-information tactic graph**: 59% of all possible tactic
  transitions exist, so the *topology* of the tactic FSM says little; only the
  *weights* carry signal.
- **Reporting/survivorship bias**: the corpus is notable, well-documented,
  largely-successful incidents — a biased sample of adversary behaviour.
- **Weight ≠ probability/efficacy** (§4): cannot be used as a transition
  probability or success likelihood without assumptions the corpus does not
  license.
- **Enterprise-only scope**: ATLAS/ICS observations are dropped from the aggregate
  (kept in the per-flow layer) — see the Enterprise-only assumption in the note.

## The central question: implementation issue, or supplement?

"Thin on generalisability" is **not** an implementation issue to fix — the build
is correct. It is a corpus limitation. There are two honest responses, not
mutually exclusive, and the next session must choose the thesis's stance:

**Option A — keep the GAP purely observed; thicken by growing the corpus.**
Add more Attack Flows; the single-observation share is the gauge to watch. The
prefix gap (§2) does *not* close this way — incident reports will keep starting at
intrusion — so under this option the recon/foothold prefix is handled *outside*
the GAP (e.g., as a fixed scripted preamble in the L3 attacker, or simply scoped
out of the research question). *Pro:* the no-synthesis invariant stays pristine;
the provenance claim is unqualified. *Con:* the GAP alone cannot model
pre-intrusion phases, which is awkward if MTD efficacy against early phases is in
scope.

**Option B — supplement with literature-grounded inferred paths, in a separately
labelled layer.** Use the hand-authoring seam to add the invariant prefix
(Alshamrani's recon → foothold) and any objective-conditioned scaffolding the
corpus under-samples, **tagged with a distinct provenance** (e.g.
`source: inferred` vs `attack_flow_corpus`) so inferred edges are never conflated
with observed ones and any view can include or exclude them. *Pro:* a richer,
literature-complete adversary; pre-intrusion phases become modellable. *Con:* this
*crosses the no-synthesis invariant* — the single most load-bearing commitment in
[`../specs/01_gap_schema.md`](../specs/01_gap_schema.md) §(a). It must be a
*declared, defended, optional overlay*, never the default GAP, or the whole
"observed, not inferred" argument collapses.

**Recommended stance for the next session to argue from (disagree from this
shortlist, not from scratch):** adopt **A as the canonical GAP**, and design **B
as an explicitly-provenanced, opt-in overlay** rather than choosing one outright.
Concretely: keep `gap_v0.5.json` purely observed; specify an `inferred`
provenance tag and a "corpus-only vs corpus+inferred" *view* switch so the thesis
can run both and show the difference. This preserves the defensible core, makes
the supplementation a measurable design choice rather than a silent assumption,
and turns the prefix-gap finding into a contribution ("we show where incident-
derived intelligence is blind, and bound the effect of correcting it") instead of
a weakness. The decision is genuinely open — record whichever way it lands in
§01_gap_schema (h) open questions.

## Downstream implications (L2–L4)

- **The GAP-driven attacker measures disruption of *commonly-reported workflows*,
  not optimal adversary behaviour.** If L3 samples GAP edges by weight, it samples
  what is frequently *documented*, biased toward successful campaigns — not what
  is effective against a given MTD configuration. This is a **comparability
  boundary** that belongs alongside the MTTC divergences in
  [`../specs/metrics_semantics.md`](../specs/metrics_semantics.md): MTD-efficacy
  numbers read as "how much MTD perturbs typical observed attack workflow".
- **Weights cannot seed transition probabilities as-is.** Any stochastic traversal
  at L3 needs an explicit, documented normalisation + closed-world assumption the
  corpus does not itself justify (§4). Flag before anyone treats the GAP as a
  Markov chain.
- **A GAP-only attacker effectively starts at initial-access.** With recon in
  10/40 flows, weighted traversal will almost never begin pre-intrusion. If MTD
  against reconnaissance/resource-development is in scope, the GAP alone is
  insufficient — this is the concrete pull toward Option B.
- **Build the L3 attacker from the backbone + scaffolding, not the raw 478-edge
  graph.** The useful, defensible signal is the high-weight backbone (§1) plus the
  objective-conditioned terminal regions (§3); the single-observation tail is
  closer to noise than to behaviour for simulation purposes.

## Hard constraints

- **The no-synthesis invariant (§01_gap_schema (a)) is load-bearing.** Nothing in
  this audit licenses adding inferred edges to the canonical GAP. Any inferred
  paths are an opt-in, separately-provenanced overlay or they do not exist.
- **The build stays deterministic / byte-identical on rebuild** (README: rebuild
  differs only in `build_date`).
- **The visualiser ([`../../data/gap/_viz/`](../../data/gap/_viz/)) is untracked
  scratch**, not a deliverable; do not wire it into the pipeline or commit it
  without an explicit decision to promote it.
- **Branch / commit / push rules** per
  [`../specs/session_workflow.md`](../specs/session_workflow.md): not on `main`,
  stage-commit, never push.
- Numbers in this audit are from the 2026-05-27 Enterprise-only build (124/478/40).
  **Recompute before re-quoting** — the artefact has changed within a day before,
  and a rebuild or corpus change moves every figure here.
